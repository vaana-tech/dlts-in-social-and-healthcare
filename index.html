DLTs in Social Services and Healthcare

DLT

“A distributed ledger is a consensus of replicated, shared, and synchronized digital data geographically spread across multiple sites, countries, or institutions. There is no central administrator or centralized data storage.”
  • Definition from wikipedia
  • Umbrella term that includes blockchain technologies backing cryptocurrencies (such as bitcoin), other blockchain technologies (ethereum) and non-blockchain based distributed ledgers (corda)

Service Voucher

“Monetary instrument to facilitate public-private procurement in highly-regulated social service and health care domain through consumer empowerement.”
  • Constrain social services and healthcare domain to service vouchers
  • Definition by me
  • We will look at this definition from various angles
Monetary instrument -> Cryptocurrencies
  • Monetary instrument within the web associates strongly with cryptocurrencies
  • DLT is the technology that drives all cryptocurrencies out there
Highly-regulated domain -> Smart contracts
  • Regulation can be enforced and automated by smart contracts
  • Smart contracts is something participating parties can agree on
  • Smart contracts proceed only if all parties approve
Public - private procurement through consumer empowerment -> network of collaborators
  • Involves a rich selection of parties: Municipalities, Health Officials, Kela, Tax Office, Service Providers, consumers
  • With DLTs one can build collaboration networks where all these parties can participate

Model the domain

“Service voucher business can be modeled as a collection of smart contracts hosted in an evolving network of nodes participating in service voucher business.”

There is no one DLT

Options range from public blockchain networks to private DLTs

  • An abundance of DLT technologies out there
  • Ethereum where nodes would be rewarded with service voucher money that they can use or sell
  • Hyperledger Fabric where one would have to administer who gets access to the network and who doesn't
  • ... or perhaps we can build channels that are public and channels that are private
